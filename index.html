Currently going to school at U of I. I generally try to take on a lot ambitious projects but then realize I don't have the time for them, and put them on the backburner. I try to find solace in the little things in life.

My professional interests include IoT, system programming, communications, signal processing, and machine learning. I enjoy creating applications, but I feel that the best way to make something is to understand the mathematics and reasoning behind it. I am helping a research group at the Beckman Institute as a part of the Institute for post-harvest loss prevention. My work there is on an android App that communicates to an agriculturals sensor-hub framework that is meant to be deployed in India.

Personally, I have a wide array of interests. I enjoy juggling and learning new tricks. I play guitar, practice calligraphy, and paint in my downtime. Other than that, I contribute to any number of personal projects: an alarm clock with a nixie tube display, sabermetric machine learning models, a distributed protocol that rewards packet forwarding through blockchain-cryptocurrency.
